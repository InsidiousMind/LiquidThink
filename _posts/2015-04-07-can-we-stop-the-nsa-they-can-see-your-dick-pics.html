It's been almost 2 years since Edward Snowden first leaked information containing classified National Security Agency Operations. There was public outcry, who likes their information being stolen and looked through by a government organization? I certainly don't.

What have we done about it to stop the NSA?

Well nothing, really. Public debate right now is severely lacking. When John Oliver compares it to a turtle having sex, you know it's bad. Just look at this video for proof:

A congress woman was interrupted during a talk on Section 215 because of Justin Beiber!? Wow.

All this goes to show is what people want to hear more about. Obviously, the news station was getting more traffic from showing Justin Beibers actions than actual news. It's almost unbeliebable.

Somethings obviously wrong here. Section 215 expires June 1st, and the date is fast approaching. If there is anytime for debate, that time is now.

John Oliver did a great segment recently on Government Surveillance.

From it, arose an amazing (and hilarious) website, CanTheySeeMyDick. (alluding to the long Dick Joke John Oliver accomplishes in the video. Props to him.)

Well, can the NSA see your dick? As of now, that is a definite and resounding YES. Section 215 is just one way the NSA uses to accomplish this. There are many more provisions too, like Executive Order 12333 or Section 702.

Section 215 is being reauthorized June 1st, however. So, it makes sense to tackle that first. What is section 215, exactly? Simply, it allows

the NSA to track your calls and, in some countries, see the content of those calls. According to Edward Snowden as well as CanTheySeeMyDick:

"they can probably tell who you're sharing your junk pictures with because they're seeing who you're texting with, who you're calling."

So it allows the NSA to see the phone numbers of who you're texting and calling, and for how long. This may not sound like much, but it gives away so much; especially to a large agency such as the NSA.


Is it a U.S specific problem? Nope. The NSA spies on everything and everybody they can. There were even reports that the NSA was spying on German Chancellor Angela Merkel. In fact, the NSA has more freedom spying on people outside the US then within. It even uses this to it's advantages; if domestic information gets on a foreign server, it can intercept that information from that server. This isn't rare, either. Say you're storing something on Google Drive. Google has many servers all over the world. Your information gets passed around all the time by Google.
