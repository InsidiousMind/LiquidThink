Here Goes:

1972

Created by the Air-force out of the need of an accurate guidance system. Originally created by the Raytheon company and then expanded by Navstar.

[caption id="attachment_1104" align="alignright" width="300"] From wondersinthedark.wordpress.com[/caption]

After years of development with the best tools and researchers in the industry, and after the realization that they could use satellites to their advantage, however, GPS technology was not yet ready to use.

1983

In 1983 it was allowed by the U.S Government for public use when it became operational. This was in response to the crash of Korean Airlines Flight 007, an airplane shot down after mistakenly entering Soviet Airspace, and killing 269 passengers. This tragedy could have easily been avoided if GPS had been operational. Thus, in order to speed up the production of the technology, Reagan allowed public consumption of the product in order to entice faster production, because, well, Capitalism.

1994

At this time, the GPS system was finally completed and operational! It was used extensively by the U.S AirForce and became a crucial part of their operations. The system consists of 24 very-heavy satellites, which circle the earth and are able to beam around your location.

1996

It was not long before the common citizen was using GPS (Capitalism, again). It started out expensive, just like when any other new tech just hits the market, only being available to the upper-classes of society.

2006

Jump up 10 years later, and BAM, GPS is everywhere. From being used on Social Networks so one can update their location to all their very nice and not-creepy-at-all stalkers, to location services to save you from being lost in an unknown town, GPS has easily made us all more lazily as people; and so, innovation at it's finest. Not yet widely available on phones, however, it was not as widespread as it will be, in

GPS. GPS, everywhere. On your phone, on your computer, in your car, in your flashy new iWatch, or your Developer edition of Google Glass--GPS is literally everywhere. Be happy, because your stalker probably is.
